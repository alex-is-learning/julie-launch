This week — three small things

Alex has already started sending people to your site. These three make sure they actually book.

Put a booking link on the site ~1 hr with Claude
Right now people have to email and wait. A "book a free intro call" button catches them while the impulse is alive.
exactly how

Use Cal.com (free plan allows multiple event types — Calendly's free plan only allows one).

  1. Create two event types: Intro call — 20 min, free and Online session — 60 min. Both get a video link automatically.
  2. For in-person sessions, don't make them instantly bookable (you need to book the room) — leave those as "contact me" or use a request-type booking you confirm.
  3. Connect your Google Calendar — busy times block automatically, so you never have to update availability by hand. Your Philippines/Vietnam trip handles itself: just block it in Google Calendar.
  4. Turn on a 15–30 min buffer between sessions and 24h minimum notice.
  5. Then tell Claude, on your website code:

"Replace the contact email CTA with a link to [your cal.com URL]. Button text: 'Book a free 20-min intro call'."

Put your founding-client deal on the site, near the top ~20 min
"Start now, keep this price after I qualify" is the single strongest reason to book today instead of someday — and right now it isn't on the site at all.
exactly how

Don't frame it as a student discount (sounds like lower quality). Frame it as founding-client pricing — something like:

"Founding clients: 400 DKK per session — about a third of the usual Copenhagen rate — and you keep this price permanently after I qualify."

Put it in the Services section right next to the price, and ideally a short line in the hero too. Tell Claude to add it.

Show $ and € next to the price ~10 min
Most people seeing "400 DKK" have no idea what that is — and bounce rather than open a currency converter.
exactly how

Wherever the price appears, make it:

"400 DKK (~$60 / €54) · 60 min"

One line to Claude on the website code. Approximate is fine — the point is that an American or international visitor instantly knows the ballpark.

Big wins — this month

Free listings where people are already searching for exactly you.

Google Business Profile ~30 min · free
Puts you on Google Maps for "english speaking therapist copenhagen" — the highest-intent search there is, free.
exactly how
  1. Go to business.google.com and create a profile for your consulting room address (a rented room you actually work in qualifies — Google's restriction is only on mail-drop addresses). Your name should be visible at the location, even a small sign.
  2. Verification arrives by postcard in 1–2 weeks.
  3. Add a photo, hours, and a short bio in English and Danish — name your areas (existential, somatic, trauma, relationships) and the free intro call.
  4. When your first clients are happy, ask them for a Google review — even 3 reviews lifts you above most listings.
Apply to It's Complicated ~45 min
The platform English-speakers in Copenhagen actually use to find therapists — founded in Copenhagen, and they accept therapists in training.
exactly how

Apply at complicated.life. There's a live Copenhagen profile that says "I'm a psychotherapist in training offering therapy at a reduced price, while finishing the last year of my psychotherapy studies" — your exact situation, already accepted.

  • Lead your bio with the founding-client rate and the free intro call.
  • They like to see ~1 year of client-facing experience — your supervised training hours count.
TherapyRoute free listing ~20 min · free
Only about 11 therapists listed in all of Copenhagen — near-zero competition, no credential gate, free tier.
exactly how

Sign up at therapyroute.com — the free tier includes up to 12 client contacts a year. Reuse the same bio you write for It's Complicated.

When those are done

Good moves, no rush. Ignore this section until the ones above are ticked.

DPFO student membership 67 DKK/mo
Denmark's main psychotherapy association — and possibly a listing in their public "find a therapist" directory.
exactly how

Join via psykoterapeutforeningen.dk (you may already be eligible through SEOP — or already a member?). Email kontakt@dpfo.dk and ask whether student members appear in the public directory. Membership also helps with the Psychology Today verification below.

Psychology Today Denmark 150 DKK/mo
The biggest therapy directory brand. Worth it once DPFO membership is sorted (they verify via association membership).
exactly how

Sign up at psychologytoday.com (Denmark) — cancel anytime. The headline + first two sentences do most of the work: speak to how the person feels, not your method list, then name the founding-client rate. Tick both the somatic and existential/humanistic filters.
